The different lifestyles of these individuals refer to:

  • Dietary behavior (based on 2000 kcal average daily requirement): The respective person either follows a meat-based diet (omnivorous) or a plant-based diet (vegetarian)
  • Recycling behavior: The respective person either recycles comprehensively or does not recycle
  • Regionality of food purchases: The respective person either buys and consumes mainly regional food or also imported food items
  • Drying laundry: The respective person either dries three loads of laundry every week by air drying them or using a tumble dryer
  • Commuting to work: The respective person either commutes a total of 20km to and from work everyday, 5 days a week for 48 weeks per year by personal car or by bus
  • Traveling on vacation: The respective person either takes two medium distance flights a year (=3-6hours) or or takes the train (e.g. London-Paris or London-Switzerland)

    • The emissions for each behavior refer to the total combined emissions for an entire year , e.g. for diet this means the emissions a person emits based on their diet and average caloric needs of a person across the course of a full year

    This is how a carbon footprint of an individual looks like in our task.


    That is, in the example above, the person follows a meat-based diet, eats regional and imported food, air dries their laundry, takes the train to go on vacation, commutes every weekday to work by car, and does not recycle.
