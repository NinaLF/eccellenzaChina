Carbon Footprints


A personal carbon footprint refers to the total amount of greenhouse gases (including CO2 and methane) that are emitted into the atmosphere as a result of an individual,s behavior.
The carbon footprint thus includes emissions from driving, flying, using electricity, cooling or heating your home, purchasing goods and other sources.
In other words, a personal carbon footprint reflects an individual's total impact on the climate system.

To give you some more information about carbon footprints, you wil see some examples of carbon footprints made up of a subset of behaviors.
The different lifestyles you will see concern 6 example behaviors:

  • Dietary behavior:
    The respective person either follows a meat-based diet (omnivorous) or a plant-based diet (vegetarian)
  • Recycling behavior:
    The respective person either recycles comprehensively or does not recycle
  • Regionality of food purchases:
    The respective person either buys and consumes mainly regional food or also imported food items
  • Laundry drying:
    The respective person either dries their laundry using the dryer or air dries it based on 3 drying loads per week and the average energy mix
  • Commuting to work:
    The respective person either commutes a total of 20km to and from work everyday, 5 days a week for 48 weeks per year by personal car or by bus
  • Traveling on vacation:
    The respective person either takes two medium distance flights a year (=3-6hours) or or takes the train (e.g. London-Paris or London-Switzerland)
